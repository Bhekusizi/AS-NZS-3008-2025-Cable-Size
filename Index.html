Free AS/NZS 3008:2025 Cable Sizing Calculator for Australian & New Zealand Electricians

This free online cable sizing calculator is built to AS/NZS 3008.1.1:2025 — the current Australian and New Zealand standard for selection of cables for voltages up to 0.6/1 kV AC and 1500V DC. Published in December 2025, this edition replaced AS/NZS 3008.1.1:2017 with significant updates including renamed correction factors (formerly derating factors), new DC current carrying capacity tables, cable tray tier correction factors, and updated temperature correction methodology aligned with IEC 60364-5-52.

How to Use the Cable Size Calculator

Enter the supply voltage (single-phase 230V, three-phase 400V/415V, or DC up to 1500V), load current in amps, kilowatts, or kVA, maximum permitted voltage drop percentage, and cable run distance in metres. Select the conductor material (copper or aluminium), cable configuration (multicore or single-core, 2-core or 3/4-core), insulation type (PVC V-75, V-90, XLPE X-90, X-HF-110, elastomer R-EP-90, R-HF-110, etc.), and installation method. Choose your load type (general, motor, sensitive electronics, or harmonic) for automatic MCB curve selection. The calculator instantly returns the recommended cable size in mm², one-line circuit diagram, overload protection compliance check, and a full comparison table of all cable sizes from 1mm² to 630mm².

Two Voltage Drop Calculation Methods per AS/NZS 3008:2025

The calculator offers two voltage drop methods. The simplified mV/A/m method per Clause 4.2 uses pre-calculated values from Tables 4.14–4.31 (AC) and the new Tables 4.16, 4.18, 4.21 (DC). The impedance method per Clause 4.3 uses VD = I × L × (R·cosφ + X·sinφ) × multiplier, accounting for the actual load power factor for more accurate results. Temperature-adjusted voltage drop per Clause 4.4 recalculates resistance at the actual conductor operating temperature for optimised cable sizing. For single-phase circuits, the three-phase mV/A/m values are multiplied by 2/√3 (1.1547). For DC circuits, dedicated DC resistance tables are used with no reactance component.

AS/NZS 3008:2025 Correction Factors (Tables 3.33–3.48)

Enable correction factors to apply all relevant CFs from AS/NZS 3008:2025. The calculator covers: circuit bunching in air and enclosures (Table 3.33 with 6 arrangement types and up to 20 circuits), cable tray tier factors for multi-tier installations on perforated or solid trays (Tables 3.34–3.35, new in the 2025 edition), ambient air temperature from 15°C to 80°C (Table 3.44), ambient soil temperature from 10°C to 40°C (Table 3.45), depth of laying from 0.5m to 3.0m for buried cables (Table 3.46) and underground enclosures (Table 3.47), soil thermal resistivity from 0.8 to 3.0 °C·m/W (Table 3.48), and underground cable groups with spacing options (Tables 3.36–3.43). Each factor is shown individually with table reference, selected value, and combined product for full audit traceability.

Current Carrying Capacity (CCC) per AS/NZS 3008:2025

Current carrying capacities are taken from Tables 3.9–3.32 of AS/NZS 3008:2025 for copper and aluminium conductors with PVC (75°C), XLPE/elastomer (90°C), and high-temperature (110°C) insulation. The calculator distinguishes between 2-core cables (Tables 3.15–3.17) and 3/4-core cables (Tables 3.18–3.20) for correct CCC selection based on circuit type. The actual conductor operating temperature is calculated using Equation 4.4(2): θ_op = θ_amb + (I_o/I_R)² × (θ_R − θ_amb), showing the real operating temperature and cable utilisation percentage.

Protection Devices — MCB, MCCB, and HRC Fuses

The calculator includes 60+ protection device models from major Australian and New Zealand brands: Schneider Electric (Acti9 iC60, iC60N, iC60H, NG125, ComPact NSX with Micrologic 2/5/6/7 trip units, MasterPact MTZ), ABB (System Pro M S200/S280/S700, Tmax XT1-XT4 with Ekip Touch trip units, SACE Emax2), Siemens (5SL4/5SL6 SENTRON, 3VA1/3VA2 with ETU310-360, 3WL), Eaton (FAZ, PL9, NZM1-NZM4 with XMIC), Legrand (DX³, DPX³, DMX³), Clipsal (MAX9, ResiMAX, 4CB, PDL), NHP (MOD6), Hager (MCN, NDN, HDB, MBN DC), Terasaki (TemBreak2 S125/S250/S400/H800), Chint (NB1-63, NXM), LS Electric (BKN, ABN), and DETA. Electronic trip unit settings (Ir 0.4–1.0×In, Isd 1–12×Ir, Ii 1.5–15×In, Ig 0.2–1.0×In) are fully configurable for MCCBs with LSIG protection. HRC fuse types include gG, gL, aM, aR, and gR per IEC 60269.

Overload Protection Check per AS/NZS 3000:2018

The calculator automatically verifies the fundamental overload protection compliance rule from AS/NZS 3000:2018 Clause 2.5.3: Ib ≤ In ≤ Iz, where Ib is the design load current, In is the protective device rated current, and Iz is the cable current carrying capacity after all correction factors. A clear pass/fail indicator shows whether the protective device properly protects the cable from overload. For harmonic loads on 3-phase circuits, a warning is displayed about neutral conductor sizing per AS/NZS 3008 Clause 3.5.2.3.

Earth Fault Loop Impedance and Short-Circuit Protection

The earth fault loop impedance calculation determines Zs = source impedance + cable loop impedance (phase conductor + earth return). Source impedance can be calculated from the prospective short-circuit current (Zs = Vph / Ipsc) or entered manually. For DC circuits, loop impedance uses resistance only (no reactance). MCB magnetic trip ranges per IEC 60898-1: Curve B (3–5×In) for sensitive electronics, Curve C (5–10×In) for general distribution, Curve D (10–20×In) for motor/transformer inrush. The adiabatic short-circuit check verifies k²S² ≥ I²t to ensure the cable can withstand prospective fault current.

DC Cable Sizing for Solar PV and Battery Systems

Full DC circuit support with dedicated DC voltage drop tables from AS/NZS 3008:2025 (Tables 4.16, 4.18, 4.21), DC MCB and DC MCCB protection devices with voltage configurations from 72V to 1000V DC for solar PV applications, and DC-specific loop impedance using resistance only. DC MCB brands include Schneider Acti9 PV DC, ABB S200PV, Siemens 5SP4 PV, Eaton FAZ PV DC, Hager MBN PV, and Chint NXB PV. For PV-specific requirements, refer to AS/NZS 5033.

Parallel Conductors per AS/NZS 3008:2025

Parallel conductors are supported per Clause 3.5.2.2 and Appendix A.3. Each parallel cable is treated as a separate circuit for correction factor application. Up to four cables per phase (2×, 3×, or 4×) are supported. Total CCC equals N × CCC per cable, current per cable = I_load / N, and phase conductor impedance reduces to Z_single / N. The earth conductor is treated conservatively as a single return path.
